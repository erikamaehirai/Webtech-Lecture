What is the meaning of Internet?

Internet is the short term for "Internetwork". It is also defined as the "Network of Networks". The Internet connects millions of people around the world and is used mostly for communication and accessing information. Computers can connect with each other as long as they are connected to the Internet. The informations that travel through the internet via variety of languages that are called Protocols. Every person that is using the internet has a unique Internet Protocol(IP) address that is unique numbers that are used to identify each computer that is connected to the Internet Protocol that are communicating in a network.


History of the Internet

Year Events
1960 Modem, the first dataphone was introduced by AT&T.
1961 On March 31,1961, Leonard Kleinrock published a paper entitled "Information Flow in Large Communication Nets".
1962 Kleinrock released a paper about packetization.
The transmission of data using fixed size message blocks was suggested by Paul Baran.
JCR Licklider became the first director of IPTO and he also gave his vision of a galactic network.
1964 The report "On Distributed Communications" was published by Baran.
Kleinrock published his first book about packet nets entitled "Communication Nets: Stochastic Message Flow and Design".
1965 Lawrence G. Roberts performed the first long distance dial-up connection from Massachusetts with Tom Marill who is based in California.
The word "Packet" was coined by Donald Davies.
1966 Roberts and Marill published a paper regarding the success of the dial-up connection that they made.
Robert Taylor joined ARPA and he brought in Larry Roberts to develop the ARPANET.
1967 The 1-node NPL packet net was created by Donald Davies.
The use of a minicomputer for network packet switch was suggested by Wes Clark.
1968 On June 3, 1968, Larry Roberts published the ARPANET program plan.
1969 On April 7, 1969, the first RFC was published and it contains Host-to-Host introductory and about IMP software.
UCLA introduced the Internet on July 3, 1969.
The first data was sent from UCLA host to IMP switch on September 2, 1969.
The first commercial online service named "CpmpuServe" was established.
1970 Steve Crocker together with UCLA released the NCP.
1971 Ray Tomlinson sent the first electronic mail.
1972 ARAPANET's first public demo.
1973 The Very first Voice over Internet Protocol(VoIP) call was made.
1974 Telnet was introduced and it was considered to be the first Internet Service Provider(ISP).
1981 Because It's There Network(BITNET) was founded.
1983 TCP/IP was standardized by ARPANET.
1984 DNS was introoduced by Mockapetris and Postel.
1989 Tim Berners-Lee submitted a proposal at CERN on MArch 12, 1989 and the proposal would become the World Wide Web(WWW)
1991 The WWW was introduced on August 6, 1991 by Tim Berners-Lee.
1994 W3C(World Wide Web Consortium) was established and was headed by Tim Berners-Lee on October 1994.
1995 dot com (.com) started.
SSL protocol was developed by Netscape on February 1995
Microsoft release the Microsoft Internet Explorer on August 16, 1995
HTML 2.0 was introduced in RFC 1866 on November 24, 1995
Java was introduced by Sun Microsystems and it also announced the JavaScript on December 4, 1995.
1996 Macromedia Falsh was introduced and it is now known as Adobe Flash
CSS1 was published by W3C in December 1996.
1997 Internet2 consortium was established
IEEE released the 802.11 network which we all know as the WIFI.

XML becomes a recommendation for W3C.
